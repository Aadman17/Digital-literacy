# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

A responsible use checklist for students at VIT Bhopal University.

---

## ✅ DO's

1. **Keep your profiles professional** – Use a clear, appropriate profile photo and a bio that reflects your academic identity. Recruiters and faculty often view social media profiles.

2. **Think before you post** – Ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my professor, parent, or future employer saw this?" If the answer is no, do not post it.

3. **Use privacy settings** – Regularly review and update your privacy settings on all platforms. Limit who can see your personal posts, location, and contact information.

4. **Give credit when sharing content** – Always tag or mention the original creator when reposting articles, images, or ideas. Plagiarism applies online too.

5. **Engage constructively in online discussions** – Share your opinions respectfully. Disagreeing with someone is fine; personal attacks are not.

6. **Report harmful content** – Use the platform's built-in reporting tools if you encounter harassment, fake profiles, or inappropriate material.

7. **Update your LinkedIn regularly** – Add new skills, projects, courses, and certifications as you complete them. A current LinkedIn profile is your live CV.

8. **Verify before sharing news** – Always check whether a news story or viral post is from a credible source before sharing it with others.

---

## ❌ DON'Ts

1. **Don't share personal information publicly** – Avoid posting your phone number, home address, college ID, or daily schedule on any public platform.

2. **Don't engage with or share fake news** – Spreading misinformation, even unintentionally, can damage your reputation and harm others.

3. **Don't post in the heat of emotion** – Angry or impulsive posts are very hard to retract. Draft it, wait an hour, then decide.

4. **Don't accept friend/follow requests from strangers** – Unknown contacts may have malicious intent. Verify the identity before accepting.

5. **Don't comment negatively about your institution, faculty, or peers** – Public complaints about your college or classmates can have serious academic and professional consequences.

6. **Don't overshare your location in real time** – Posting "live" locations or check-ins tells strangers exactly where you are, which is a safety risk.

7. **Don't click suspicious links in DMs** – Phishing scams frequently arrive as messages from fake accounts claiming to offer prizes, jobs, or discounts.

8. **Don't use social media during lectures or exams** – Apart from the academic impact, it demonstrates disrespect to faculty and is increasingly subject to disciplinary action.

---

*This checklist was created as part of the CSE0001 Digital Literacy Project, VIT Bhopal University.*
