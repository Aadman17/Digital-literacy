# Stay Safe Online – Cybercrime Prevention Checklist
### For College Students in India

---

## General Online Safety

1. **Use strong, unique passwords for every account.**
   Do not use your name, date of birth, or "1234" as a password. Use a mix of uppercase letters, numbers, and symbols. Consider using a password manager.

2. **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on all important accounts.**
   This includes your email, social media, and banking apps. Even if your password is stolen, 2FA prevents unauthorised access.

3. **Never click on links in unsolicited emails, WhatsApp messages, or SMS.**
   Phishing links often appear as bank alerts, prize notifications, or government messages. Always visit the official website directly.

4. **Verify the identity of anyone who contacts you online claiming to be from a bank, company, or government body.**
   Legitimate organisations will never ask for your password, OTP, or PIN over call, chat, or email.

5. **Keep your devices and apps updated.**
   Software updates often contain security patches. Delayed updates leave your device vulnerable to known exploits.

6. **Do not download apps from unofficial sources.**
   Only use Google Play Store or Apple App Store. Unofficial APK files frequently contain malware.

---

## Financial / UPI Safety *(Mandatory)*

7. **Remember: You NEVER need to enter your UPI PIN to receive money.**
   A QR code + UPI PIN always means money is leaving your account. If someone asks you to do this to "receive" a payment, it is a scam. Disconnect immediately.

8. **Never share your UPI PIN, CVV, card number, or OTP with anyone — including people claiming to be from your bank.**
   Banks will never ask for this information. Anyone who does is a fraudster. Report them immediately.

---

## Social Media & Identity Safety

9. **Do not accept friend or follow requests from people you do not know.**
   Fake profiles are frequently used to gather personal information for identity theft or social engineering.

10. **Regularly audit what personal information is publicly visible on your profiles.**
    Check your privacy settings on Instagram, Facebook, and LinkedIn. Your phone number and location should never be publicly visible.

---

## If You Are a Victim

11. **Report cybercrime immediately at the National Cyber Crime Portal: [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)**

12. **Call the National Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930 (available 24x7)**
    For financial fraud, calling 1930 immediately and providing your transaction ID may help freeze the fraudulent transaction before funds are transferred further.
