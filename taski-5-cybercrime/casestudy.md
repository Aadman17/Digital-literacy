# Task 5 – Cybercrime Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

**Cybercrime Type:** UPI / Online Payment Fraud
**Case Type:** Realistic Fictional Scenario (based on widely documented fraud patterns in India)

---

## Case Study

### Background

Rohan, a 19-year-old first-year engineering student at a university in Bhopal, received a WhatsApp message from an unknown number claiming to be from a popular e-commerce platform. The message stated that Rohan had won a prize of ₹5,000 and that to "claim" it, he simply needed to scan a QR code and enter his UPI PIN to verify his account.

### How the Fraud Happened (Step by Step)

1. **Initial Contact:** Rohan received a message congratulating him on winning a reward. The message looked official, with a company logo and professional formatting.

2. **Building Trust:** The fraudster sent a fake "winner certificate" and asked Rohan to confirm his name and UPI ID — information Rohan shared willingly, not realising it would be misused.

3. **The Trap:** Rohan was sent a QR code and told to "scan it to receive the money." He was not told that scanning the QR code would initiate a payment *from* his account, not to it.

4. **Execution of Fraud:** Rohan scanned the code and entered his UPI PIN. Within seconds, ₹4,800 was debited from his account. The fraudster immediately blocked his number.

5. **Aftermath:** Rohan discovered he had been defrauded only when he checked his bank notification. The transaction was irreversible.

### Who Is Usually Targeted

First-time internet users, students with newly opened bank accounts, and anyone unfamiliar with how UPI payments work. Fraudsters deliberately target people who might not know that a QR code combined with a UPI PIN always results in *money leaving* your account.

### Consequences

Rohan lost money that his parents had sent for his monthly expenses. Beyond the financial loss, he experienced anxiety and embarrassment. Reporting the fraud was possible via the National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in) and Helpline **1930**, but recovery of funds is not guaranteed.

### Key Lesson

**You never need to enter your UPI PIN to receive money.** If anyone tells you to scan a QR code or enter your PIN to "receive" a payment, it is a scam.
